## Weekly Publishing Schedule

| Day | Feed post | Branch | Effort | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Founder Talking Head — "This week's trip" | 1 | 🟡 Medium | Plan Sunday, film Mon AM, post Mon PM |
| **Tue** | Trip Postcard (atmospheric Reel) | A | 🟢 Easy | 30-min edit from guide footage |
| **Wed** | River Spotlight (blog + carousel crosspost) | E | 🟡 Medium | Written Sun-Tue, posted Wed |
| **Thu** | Trip Postcard | A | 🟢 Easy | — |
| **Fri** | Carousel Teach-Up | B | 🟢 Easy | Written Wed, designed Thu |
| **Sat** | Field Notes (written or founder voice) | 3 | 🟡 Medium | Written Fri |
| **Sun** | Trip Postcard *or* heavy-asset cutdown | A or 2/F | 🟢/🔴 | Replaced once per month by Wild Report or First Salmon Story drop |

**Stories run daily** — separately from feed. Repurposed clips, behind-the-scenes, polls, river-of-the-day, "ask me anything." Stories carry the daily relationship; feed posts carry the brand.

**Monthly heavy drops:** One Sunday per month replaces the Postcard with a **Wild Report** (Branch 2). A different Sunday drops a **First Salmon Story** (Branch F). One long-form asset fuels 4-5 atomized cutdowns over the following 2 weeks.

## The 7 Branches

### Branch 1 — Founder Talking Head

**Pillar:** 2. Behind the Guides (founder-fronted before guides take stage)
**Format:** Vertical video, 30-60 sec
**Cadence:** 1 / week (Monday)
**Effort:** 🟡 Medium

**Why this branch exists.** Pre-launch trust is built by faces, not logos. Founders' faces on camera every Monday creates recognition before any guide footage is asked of the audience. Once trust is built (~Month 3+), guides can take over the slot.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook (3 sec)** — *"This is [specific river] in [specific week]."*
2. **The trip (15-20 sec)** — what it is, when it fishes, what species, who guides it
3. **The personal moment (10-15 sec)** — one specific story, fish, or pool
4. **CTA (5 sec)** — *"Link in bio. We book this with [guide name]. 4 spots left."*

**Production rules:**
- Always shot on/near a river — not in a kitchen, not in front of a logo
- River audio audible. No music over founder's voice.
- One founder per video. Rotate Tymon / Krzychu.
- Subtitles burned in, English.

**First 5 to film:**
1. Krzychu introducing the Gaula in Week 26
2. The Norwegian river that's still open in 2026 (counter-positioning vs the 33 closed)
3. *"The cheapest serious salmon week we book — and why we book it"*
4. *"The most expensive week we book — and what makes it worth it"*
5. Iceland — Mýrarkvísl or another lesser-known river vs the famous ones

**Words to use:** wild, beat, pool, run, vetted, week, worth it, 4 spots, season opens
**Words to avoid:** package, deal, exclusive, luxury, exotic, world-class

### Branch 2 — Wild Reports

**Pillar:** 1. Nordic Nature (the spine, made explicit)
**Format:** 8-12 min YouTube + 60-90s IG Reel cutdowns + blog post + email feature
**Cadence:** 1 / month (Sunday drop)
**Effort:** 🔴 Hard

**Why this branch exists.** This is the conservation spine made tangible. It's the "care for nature" value earned, not claimed. One asset = four placements = compounding authority over time.

**Production rules:**
- Founders on camera + B-roll + simple animation/graphics for data
- *Not* slick documentary. Real Talk = a bit rough is fine. Real beats polished.
- Every video has a sourced number, a sourced quote, and a named expert reference (we cite, like a journalist)
- Always end with: *what we do about it* + *what you can do*

**Structure:**
1. **Cold open** — one shocking number or visual (*"In 2024, Norway closed 33 salmon rivers."*)
2. **The problem (3-4 min)** — what's actually happening
3. **The mechanism (2-3 min)** — how it actually works, with simple graphics
4. **What's being done (1-2 min)** — NGOs, regulators, conservationists
5. **What we do (1 min)** — strict C&R on impacted rivers, river selection, etc.
6. **What you can do (30 sec)** — specific action

**Roadmap of first 5 reports:**
1. **The 33 Rivers** — why Norway closed mid-season in 2024 and what it means for 2026 (first drop)
2. **The Sea Lice Math** — how one farm's lice travel 30km and kill wild smolt
3. **The Pink Salmon Invasion** — the invasive species crowding out Atlantic salmon in Nordic rivers
4. **The Hemsila Story** — *"What saved Hemsila was that when the fish disappeared, the fishermen also disappeared."* Recovery story.
5. **Sápmi & Tana** — the indigenous-rights angle to Nordic salmon that no one is engaging with

**Distribution after drop:**
- Day 1: YouTube + blog
- Day 2-7: 4-5 IG Reel cutdowns
- Week 2: Newsletter feature
- Week 3-4: Repurposed Stories + atomized carousels

### Branch 3 — Field Notes

**Pillar:** 5. Real Talk
**Format:** Written-first — IG carousels, LinkedIn, blog, occasional founder voice-overs
**Cadence:** 1 / week (Saturday)
**Effort:** 🟡 Medium

**Why "Field Notes" not "build in public."** SaaS build-in-public is metrics, MRR, "I just hit $10K." That tone-clashes with a Wild/Rooted/Real Nordic brand. Field Notes is the angler/naturalist tradition reframed — founders as scouts and observers, not founders as startup posters.

**What it covers:**
- **Vetting transparency** — *"We rejected 3 guides this week. Here's why."*
- **Honest "no's"** — *"This river makes us money. We won't book it this season."*
- **Founder reflections** — *"6 months in: the messy parts no one shows."*
- **Industry critique** — *"What 'guided fishing' usually means — and what we mean."*
- **Travel journals** — *"3 days, 2 rivers, 1 guide we'll book. Field notes from Lofoten."*
- **Lessons learned** — *"We almost shipped a trip that wasn't ready. Here's what stopped us."*

**What it does NOT cover:**
- MRR / revenue / metrics (off-brand)
- "We hit X followers!" (vanity)
- Generic "behind the scenes" without an actual point
- Anything that sounds like a founder LinkedIn post

**Voice constraint:** First person, plural ("we") or singular ("I"). Conversational. Honest about limits. Never breathless. Never hashtag-bro.

**First 5 to write:**
1. *"We rejected 3 guides this month. Here's the bar."*
2. *"The most beautiful river we're not booking in 2026 — and why."*
3. *"What 6 months of building this looks like."*
4. *"The scariest moment of a trip is not what you think."*
5. *"We're Polish students who fish Norway. People ask what we know that locals don't. Honest answer:"*

### Branch A — Trip Postcards

**Pillar:** 3. Destinations + spine carrying
**Format:** Vertical Reel, 15-30 sec. **No talking.** Just shots + on-screen text + ambient sound.
**Cadence:** 3 / week (Tue, Thu, Sun)
**Effort:** 🟢 Easy

**Why this branch exists.** Pure desire content. Sells the dream without selling. This is what fills the feed between heavier branches and what gets shared most. The lowest-effort, highest-volume slot.

**Structure:**
- **6-8 clips, 2-4 sec each** — water, mist, fly box, casting, fish, midnight light, river
- **On-screen text:** river name + week + one detail (*"Senja. Saturday. 11pm light."*)
- **Sound:** real river audio + maybe one music track (no voiceover)
- **End frame:** small logo + tagline

**Sources of footage:**
- Guide phone footage (must request from all 20 guides as part of onboarding)
- Founder trips (Krzychu's existing library)
- Stock B-roll only as last resort (and must be Nordic — no Patagonian misfires)

**5 atmospheric angles to recur:**
1. *Pre-dawn river* — mist, no humans, fly being tied in foreground
2. *The cast* — silhouette + line in air + river behind
3. *The hold* — fish in water, hands wet, no fish-out-of-water hero shots
4. *Midnight sun* — late-summer Nordic light over water
5. *The walk-in* — boots + river + path

**Words on screen — keep to one detail:** river name, week number, weather, water temp, distance from town. *Not* marketing copy.

### Branch B — Carousel Teach-Ups

**Pillar:** 4. The Craft
**Format:** 8-10 slide IG carousel. One concept per post. Big bold text on each slide.
**Cadence:** 1 / week (Friday)
**Effort:** 🟢 Easy

**Why this branch exists.** Highest save rate of any IG format. Educational = trust. No video skills needed. Saves stack into a content library that anglers come back to. Doubles as repurposable email content and lead-magnet seed material.

**Structure (10-slide template):**
1. **Hook slide** — *"5 things every angler gets wrong about Atlantic salmon."*
2. **Setup slide** — why it matters (the cost of getting it wrong)
3-7. **5 points**, one per slide, each with one image or visual
8. **The "if you take one thing"** slide
9. **Source / further reading** slide
10. **Tagline + soft CTA** — *"Wild Nordic. Real guides. fjordanglers.com"*

**10 starter topics (each = one carousel):**
1. *5 things every angler gets wrong about Atlantic salmon*
2. *How to read a Norwegian salmon pool*
3. *What to pack for a Nordic week (the actual list)*
4. *Why we rotate pools — and why you should care*
5. *Catch-and-release done right (5 mistakes to avoid)*
6. *Norwegian salmon flies that work — and why*
7. *What 'guided fishing' actually means in Norway vs Iceland vs Sweden*
8. *Reading water temperature — what the numbers mean for your week*
9. *What sea lice are and why they matter to your trip*
10. *Booking a Nordic trip: the questions to ask any operator*

**Save trigger:** every carousel ends with one practical takeaway worth bookmarking. Without that, save rate dies.

### Branch E — River Spotlights

**Pillar:** 3. Destinations + **SEO engine**
**Format:** Long-form blog (800-1200 words) + matching IG carousel + 60-90s Reel cutdown
**Cadence:** 1 / week (Wednesday)
**Effort:** 🟡 Medium

**Why this branch exists.** Catches the buyer at the moment they Google *"best fly fishing river Norway"* — high-intent search traffic. Each post is a long-term SEO asset that compounds. Year 1 = 50+ rivers indexed.

**Structure (blog post):**
1. **H1:** *"Fly fishing the [River Name] — what we book, when, and what to expect (2026)"*
2. **Lede (50-100 words)** — one sharp paragraph about why this river matters
3. **At a Glance table** — species, season, average price/week, current status, our take
4. **The river** (300-400 words) — geography, water character, what's special
5. **The fishing** (300-400 words) — when it fishes, what species when, technique
6. **Conservation status** (100-200 words) — current state, threats, our policy
7. **Who we book it with** (50-100 words) — guide profile reference
8. **What it costs** (50 words) — transparent range
9. **Inquire CTA** — link to inquiry form

**SEO target keywords (one per post):**
- *"fly fishing Gaula"* / *"Gaula salmon fishing"*
- *"Mýrarkvísl Iceland"* / *"Iceland salmon rivers"*
- *"fly fishing Senja"* / *"Senja sea trout"*
- Plus the German + Polish equivalents on dedicated pages later

**10 starter rivers to spotlight:**
1. Gaula (Norway) — the iconic
2. Orkla (Norway) — the alternative
3. Namsen (Norway) — the trophy water
4. Bjerkreimselva (Norway) — VOC referenced
5. Senja (Norway) — sea trout VOC referenced
6. Mýrarkvísl (Iceland) — VOC referenced
7. Eira (Norway) — VOC referenced
8. The Hemsila (Norway) — recovery story
9. The Tana (Norway/Finland) — closure context, indigenous angle
10. Em Storå (Sweden) — the Sweden play

**Cross-format atomization:**
- **Blog** → primary SEO asset
- **Carousel** → 8 slides extracting the *"At a Glance"* + *"What it costs"*
- **Reel** → 60s cutdown, founder voice-over the river facts with B-roll
- **Email** → River of the month feature

### Branch F — First Salmon Stories

**Pillar:** 1. Nordic Nature + 5. Real Talk
**Format:** Long-form interview/profile (written) + photo essay + optional Reel cutdown
**Cadence:** 1 / month (Sunday drop, alternates with Wild Reports)
**Effort:** 🔴 Hard

**Why this branch exists.** Hits the strongest universal VOC emotion: *"first salmon" / "erster Lachs" / "pierwszy łosoś."* Every angler has the story. Telling other anglers' stories well builds emotional brand depth that no other operator does.

**Structure:**
1. **Hero photo** — angler + first salmon (or just the river, if pre-launch)
2. **Lede** — one paragraph capturing the moment
3. **Setup** — who this person is, what they do, what fishing means to them
4. **The years before** — preparation, the dreaming, the gear-acquiring
5. **The trip** — practical: where, when, with whom
6. **The moment** — the take, the fight, the landing, the feeling
7. **What changed** — what they learned, what they'd do differently
8. **Where they go now** — what's next

**Pre-launch source pool:**
- Tymon's first Atlantic salmon
- Krzychu's first Atlantic salmon
- Lukas's first Atlantic salmon
- 2-3 anglers from your network (forum acquaintances, guide contacts)

**Post-launch:** Customer-sourced. After every 5 trips, we have 5 candidates.

**Length:** 1500-2500 words. Long enough to feel like an article, short enough to read in one sitting.

**Distribution:**
- Blog (primary)
- 1 Reel cutdown — the moment
- 3-5 Stories — pull quotes
- Newsletter feature
- Eventually: print zine compilation as lead magnet

## Atomization Rules

**Every heavy asset feeds the easy formats. Without atomization, the system collapses.**

| Heavy asset | Atomized into |
|---|---|
| 1 Wild Report (Branch 2) | 4-5 Reel cutdowns + 1 carousel summary + 5-7 Stories + 1 email feature |
| 1 River Spotlight (Branch E) | 1 carousel + 1 Reel + 3-4 Stories + 1 email mention |
| 1 First Salmon Story (Branch F) | 1 Reel cutdown + 3-5 Story pull-quotes + 1 newsletter feature |
| 1 founder trip / vetting visit | 1 Field Notes post + 2-3 Trip Postcards + Stories all week |

**Rule of thumb:** every piece of original work should fuel a minimum of 4 placements. If it doesn't, it's underused.

## Voice Non-Negotiables

These are the rules every post must pass *before* it ships:

1. **Spine filter:** Did this teach the audience something about respecting Nordic waters? If no — rewrite or kill.
2. **Voice filter:** Would I send this to a fishing buddy without being embarrassed? If no — rewrite.
3. **Word filter:** Does it use any of the forbidden words? *(luxury, exclusive, world-class, package, exotic, white-glove, premium experience, bucket list, adventure-seeker, traveler)* If yes — rewrite.
4. **Person filter:** Is it written in first person plural ("we") or singular ("I")? Brand-third-person is forbidden. *Never* "Fjord Anglers offers…"
5. **Honesty filter:** Does it overpromise? *"You will catch fish"* fails. *"Here's the math on a typical week"* passes.

**Voice mantra:** *Real Talk over polish. Wild over premium. Founder over brand.*

## When to Adjust This Plan

Revisit this doc:
- After 30 days — drop any branch with <50 reach/post or <5 saves average
- After 90 days — promote whichever branch is driving the most inquiries to 2x cadence
- After first long-form launch (Branch 2) — assess if the production is sustainable; if not, drop to quarterly
- When a content hire is added — promote heavy branches first (Wild Reports → biweekly)
